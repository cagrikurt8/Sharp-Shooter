Description

Let’s implement logic in our game. Take a look at the figure below:


The game comprises three stages:

  1. START. At this stage, we prepare all required variables (for instance, the Number of attempts), the game screen, and limit the movement of the Gun Sight. Go to the next level by pressing the space bar;
  2. GAME. That's where the actual gameplay starts — register keyboard events and move the Gun Sight. Every time players press the space bar, add a Bullet Hole on the Canvas and decrement the Number of attempts variable. When the Number of attempts reaches zero, move to the next stage;
  3. END. Gameplay stops here. Stop the Gun Sight movement and no more Bullet Holes. Put the final results on the screen.

Speaking of the results, we need to evaluate the shooting accuracy. It is logical to assume that the closer you get to the center of the target, the higher the reward. Accordingly, a hit in the central circle scores 10 points; the last outer circle gives 1 point. The circles are in inverse proportion to their distances from the center. The current information is displayed on the Statusbar.

Objectives

Limit the gameplay to 12 attempts. The requirements of on-screen graphical objects remain the same as in the previous stages. For testing purposes, the initial position of the Gun Sight must be at the center of the Target. Display the information about the game state in the Statusbar as follows:

  • At the START, display the following text: Press the SPACE bar to start the game;
  • During the GAME phase, display Bullets left: {1}, your score: {2} ({3}). Where {1} is the number of remaining attempts, {2} is the total score, {3} is the last score;
  • In the END, display Game over, your score: {1}. Where {1} is a total score.
